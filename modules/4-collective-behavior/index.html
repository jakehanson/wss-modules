Collective Behavior

Instructions

In this simulation, you will be playing with a stripped down version of nest site selection in Temnothorax ant colonies. Your goal is to reverse engineer the rule by which the colony comes to a collective decision. As you will see, there is a progress bar to the left and right of the arena that keeps track of how close a colony is to choosing a given nest. When the progress bar fills up, a consensus decision has been reached. To aid in your understanding, a list of helpful questions is provided.

Questions

  1. What does the ant color represent?
  2. When does the progress bar for a given nest increase?
  3. Use your answers from questions 1-2 to reverse engineer the "rule" that is being used to choose between nests. What is it?
  4. How does the nest geometry affect the outcome of the collective decision given this rule? Specifically, will the colony choose a smaller or larger nest? What about a smaller or larger aperture size?
  5. How might evolution "program" individual-level behavior in order to select nests with a specific geometry?
